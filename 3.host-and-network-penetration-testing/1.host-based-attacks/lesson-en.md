# Introduction To System/Host Based Attacks

- System/host-based attacks target a specific host or operating system, such as Windows or Linux.

- In penetration testing, the attack surface is not limited to network services; host configurations and local weaknesses can also be targeted.

- These attacks are often performed after gaining access to a target network, with the goal of compromising servers, workstations, or laptops on the internal network.

---

- System/host-based attacks focus on exploiting vulnerabilities and misconfigurations in the target operating system.

- Compared with network-based attacks, host-based attacks are more specialized and require a solid understanding of the target OS and its weaknesses.

- Common techniques include exploiting unpatched software, weak configurations, poor service settings, and other host-level flaws.

- In the next lessons, we will focus on Windows and Linux vulnerabilities and how they can be exploited.
